# Explaining Liuyao to International Users

Avoid presenting Liuyao as superstition or certainty. The best framing is symbolic decision reflection.

Recommended language:

> Liuyao is an ancient Chinese six-line oracle. It does not remove uncertainty, but it gives uncertainty a symbolic shape: the present pattern, the changing pressure, the relationship between self and world, and the direction that deserves attention.

Key translation choices:

- 本卦: Primary Hexagram
- 变卦: Changed Hexagram
- 互卦: Mutual Hexagram
- 动爻: Moving Line
- 世爻: Shi Line, the querent's position
- 应爻: Ying Line, the counterpart or outside condition
- 用神: Useful God, the symbolic focus of the question

The ideal tone is mystical but not vague, ancient but not alienating, practical but not clinical.
